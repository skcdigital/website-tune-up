5 Excel Tricks That Save SA Spaza & Salon Owners 4 Hours a Week

By Suzan · 7 min read · Pretoria, ZA

If you're still adding up daily takings on a calculator or counting stock with pen and paper, this post is for you. These 5 Excel formulas turn one afternoon a week into 5 minutes a day.

You don't need to be a "computer person". Open Microsoft Excel or free Google Sheets — both work the same. Type the formula exactly as shown (the = sign tells the spreadsheet "this is a formula, not text").

1SUM — for daily takings & weekly totals

Stop adding numbers in your head. If your daily sales for the week are in cells B2 to B8, the total is:

=SUM(B2:B8)

Drag this down for every week, and your monthly total is just =SUM(B2:B32). This alone saves most owners 2 hours a week.

2IF — flag low stock automatically

Set up a column with your stock count and use IF to scream when something is running out. If C2 is your current stock, this writes "REORDER" when stock drops below 5:

=IF(C2<5,"REORDER","ok")
BONUS Highlight the column → Home → Conditional Formatting → "Highlight cells equal to" → REORDER → red fill. Now low stock screams at you in red.

3VLOOKUP — instant price lookup

Tired of scrolling to find the price of "Brazilian 18inch" or "Maizena 1kg"? Set up a price list on Sheet 2 (column A = product, column B = price). On Sheet 1 type the product name in A2, then in B2:

=VLOOKUP(A2, Sheet2!A:B, 2, FALSE)

The price appears instantly. Perfect for quick quotes on WhatsApp without flipping pages.

4SUMIF — total sales per product or per day

Want to know how much you made on shampoos this month? If column A is the product name and column B is the sale amount:

=SUMIF(A:A, "Shampoo", B:B)

That gives you total Shampoo sales. Change "Shampoo" to any product and you'll see your real best-sellers — usually not the ones you think.

5TODAY & date math — auto-track what's due

Useful for stokvel collections, customer accounts, or supplier payments. If D2 is the due date:

=D2 - TODAY()

You'll see how many days until (or since) the due date. Combine with IF for an alert:

=IF(D2-TODAY()<0,"OVERDUE","ok")

The 4-hour saving — where does it come from?

  • Daily takings totals: ~2 hrs/week saved
  • Stock checks & reorder: ~1 hr/week saved
  • Price lookups during the day: ~30 min/week saved
  • End-of-month sales report: ~30 min/week saved

That's ~4 hours a week back in your life. Use it to serve more customers, or to actually rest.
