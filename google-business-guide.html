Get Your Business On Google Maps in 7 Simple Steps

A free, plain-English setup guide for South African small businesses. No tech jargon — just the exact steps to start showing up when locals search for you.

1

Go to Google Business Profile

Open google.com/business and click Manage now. Sign in with the Gmail account you want to own this profile (use your business email if you have one).

TIP
Use one Gmail you'll never lose access to. This account "owns" your listing.
2

Enter Your Business Name & Category

Type your exact business name (e.g. "Kwinika's Salon"). Pick the category that best matches what you do — like Hair salon, Plumber, Caterer, IT services.

  • Don't stuff keywords into your name (Google will suspend you).
  • You can add more categories later.
3

Add Your Location (or Service Area)

If customers visit you (shop, salon, office), add your physical address. If you go to them (plumber, mobile hairdresser), choose "I deliver goods and services to my customers" and pick the suburbs/cities you serve.

SA TIP
For service areas, list specific suburbs (Pretoria CBD, Centurion, Hatfield) — not just "Gauteng".
4

Add Phone & Website

Use a phone number you actually answer. If you don't have a website yet, you can use your Facebook page link, or come back and add one later (we can build you a R1,500 starter site that links here perfectly).

5

Verify Your Business

Google needs to confirm you're real. They'll offer one or more options:

  • Postcard — they post a code to your address (5–14 days in SA).
  • Phone / SMS — instant code via call or text.
  • Video verification — record a short video of your premises, signage, and ID.

Pick whichever they offer. Don't change your business info until verification is complete — it can reset the process.

6

Complete Your Profile (This Is Where Most People Quit)

A complete profile shows up 7× more than a half-filled one. Add:

  • Business hours (mark public holidays!)
  • A short description (what you do + who you help)
  • At least 5 real photos — outside, inside, team, products/work, logo
  • Services or products with prices
  • Payment methods you accept (EFT, cash, card, SnapScan)
7

Get Your First 5 Reviews

Reviews are the #1 thing that makes you rank above competitors. After verification, Google gives you a short review link in your dashboard.

  • Send the link via WhatsApp to your last 10 happy customers.
  • Ask them by name: "Hi Thandi, would you mind leaving a quick Google review? Here's the link 🙏"
  • Reply to every review — even bad ones. Politely.
SA TIP
Never pay for fake reviews. Google detects them and will suspend your profile permanently.
