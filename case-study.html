The Problem

The owner — let's call him Thabo — runs a 3-person plumbing crew in Pretoria East. His business was busy. Too busy, actually. He was getting 4 to 6 quote requests a day from WhatsApp, calls and Google.

The bottleneck wasn't the work. It was the quoting.

Before · 90 min per quote

  • Open a Word template
  • Re-type customer details every time
  • Look up part prices in 3 different places
  • Calculate VAT and labour by hand
  • Forget to add callout fees
  • Save as PDF, attach to WhatsApp
  • Often quoted same job twice with different prices

After · 8 min per quote

  • Open one Excel file
  • Pick parts from a dropdown (auto-prices)
  • Pick labour type (auto-rate)
  • VAT, callout & totals calculated live
  • Customer details auto-fill from the lead row
  • One click → branded PDF saved
  • Quote number & history logged automatically

What We Built

One Excel workbook. No software subscription. No new system to learn. Just three sheets:

  • Quote sheet — clean, branded layout with dropdowns. Print-ready as PDF in one click.
  • Pricelist sheet — Thabo updates prices here once a month. The quote sheet pulls from it automatically.
  • Quote register — every quote is auto-logged with date, customer, total, and status (pending / won / lost). Filterable.

The clever bits

  • Conditional formatting flags any quote with a margin under 25%.
  • A "Convert to invoice" button copies the quote into an invoice sheet with one click and bumps the invoice number.
  • Built-in callout, after-hours and weekend rate logic — no more forgetting the surcharge.
  • Works offline. No internet, no problem. The crew can quote on-site from a tablet.
The math

4 quotes/day × 82 minutes saved per quote = 5h 28m saved per day. Even at a conservative R150/hour billable rate, that's roughly R12,000 of extra capacity per month — for a once-off R1,200 build.

The Result

Within the first 2 weeks, Thabo:

  • Sent quotes the same day instead of "I'll get back to you tomorrow"
  • Won 3 jobs he would have lost to faster competitors
  • Stopped under-quoting (the calc forces VAT & callout)
  • Started quoting from his bakkie between jobs
"I thought I needed an app. Suzan built me one Excel file and now I quote in 5 minutes. I won 3 jobs in the first 2 weeks just because I sent the quote first." — Thabo, Plumbing business owner · Pretoria East

Why Excel, Not an App?

A subscription quoting app would have cost him R400/month forever, plus a learning curve for staff who are already on the tools all day. The Excel tool:

  • Cost R1,200 once.
  • Runs on the laptop, phone or tablet he already owns.
  • Belongs to him — no vendor lock-in, no monthly fee, no internet needed.
  • Can be edited or extended later (he's already asked for a stock module).

  • Quoting or invoicing manually
  • Tracking stock, jobs, deliveries, or staff hours in WhatsApp messages
  • Re-typing the same info into different templates
  • Losing track of who owes what
